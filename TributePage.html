The Nightingale of India

Sarojini Naidu

1879-1949

Here's a time line of Sarojini Naidu's life:

  • 1879 - Born in Hyderabad, India
  • 1891 - Naidu passed her matriculation examination to qualify for university study, earning the highest rank, when she was twelve.
  • 1895 to 1898 - Naidu studied in England, at King's College, London and then Girton College, Cambridge, with a scholarship from the Nizam of Hyderabad.She meets the poet Edmund Gosse, a professor at Cambridge University who helps her improve her skills at poetry.
  • 1904 - Naidu became an increasingly popular orator, promoting Indian independence and women's rights, especially women's education.
  • 1905 - Naidu addressed the Indian National Congress and the Indian Social Conference in Calcutta.Her first book of poems was published in London , titled "The Golden Threshold". The publication was suggested by Edmund Gosse, and bore an introduction by Arthur Symons. It also included a sketch of Naidu as a teenager, in a ruffled white dress, drawn by John Butler Yeats.
  • 1912 - Naidu's second and most strongly nationalist book of poems, The Bird of Time, was published in 1912. It was published in both London and New York, and includes "In the Bazaars of Hyderabad".
  • 1914 - Naidu met Mahatma Gandhi, whom she credited with inspiring a new commitment to political action.
  • 1917 - With Muthulakshmi Reddy, she helped established the Women's Indian Association. Later that year, Naidu accompanied her colleague Annie Besant, who was the president of Home Rule League and Women's Indian Association, to advocate universal suffrage in front of the Joint Select Committee in London, United Kingdom.She also supported the Lucknow Pact, a joint Hinduâ€“Muslim demand for British political reform, at the Madras Special Provincial Council.
  • 1925 - Naidu was the first Indian female president of the Indian National Congress.
  • 1930 - When Gandhi was arrested on 6 April 1930, he appointed Naidu as the new leader of the Salt March campaign.
  • 1931 - Naidu and other leaders of the Congress Party participated in the Second Round Table Conference headed by Viceroy Lord Irwin in the wake of the Gandhi-Irwin pact.
  • 1932 - Naidu was jailed by the British.
  • 1942 - The British jailed Naidu for her participation in the Quit India Movement.She was imprisoned for 21 months.
  • 1947 - Following India's independence from the British rule in 1947, Naidu was appointed the governor of the United Provinces (present-day Uttar Pradesh), making her India's first woman governor.
  • 1949 - Naidu remained in office until her death in March 1949 (aged 70).

Naidu is known as "one of India's feminist luminaries". Naidu's birthday, 13 February, is celebrated as Women's Day to recognise powerful voices of women in India's history.
